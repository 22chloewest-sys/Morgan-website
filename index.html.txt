    <h2>Morgan State: A Historic Journey</h2>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Origins and Early Mission:</strong> Established in 1867 as the Centenary Biblical Institute to train young men for ministry.</li>
      <li><strong>Renaming and Honor:</strong> Renamed Morgan College in 1890 to honor Reverend Lyttleton Morgan for his contributions.</li>
      <li><strong>Modern Growth and Diversity:</strong> Achieved university status in 1975, expanding programs and embracing diversity.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section id="evolution">
    <h2>Evolution of Health Sciences</h2>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Early Program Development:</strong> In the early 2010s, MSU began building its Health Sciences programs with strong faculty and planning.</li>
      <li><strong>Launch of Initial Programs:</strong> By 2015, launched Public Health and Allied Health programs to meet community needs.</li>
      <li><strong>Expansion and Leadership:</strong> Recent years brought new degrees, partnerships, and facilities that strengthened MSU’s leadership in health education.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section id="infrastructure">
    <h2>Health Sciences Infrastructure</h2>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Early 2000s Modernization:</strong> Major investments in new laboratories and research facilities.</li>
      <li><strong>2017 Milestone:</strong> Opening of the Martin D. Jenkins Behavioral and Social Sciences Center.</li>
      <li><strong>Recent Enhancements:</strong> Introduction of simulation labs and interdisciplinary research spaces.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section id="programs">

    </ul>
  </section>

      <li><strong>Leadership in Public Health:</strong> Committed to promoting wellness and equity globally.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
